Pythagorean mathematics was far more than a set of geometric rules; it was a holistic "theory of everything" that treated numbers as the fundamental substance of reality. For Pythagoras and his followers, mathematics served as a bridge between the physical and spiritual worlds, governing everything from the movement of planets to the state of the human soul.
## The Core Tenet: "All is Number"
The Pythagoreans believed that numbers (specifically positive integers) were not just tools for measurement but the "first principles" of the entire cosmos. They viewed the universe as a Kosmos—a term they popularized to describe an inherently ordered and beautiful system.

* Ontological Priority: They argued that numbers are more fundamental than physical matter; just as a shadow depends on an object, physical objects depend on numerical structures for their existence.
* The Tetractys: A sacred symbol consisting of ten points arranged in a triangle (1+2+3+4). It represented the perfection of number and the transition from a point (1) to a line (2), a plane (3), and a solid (4).
* The Table of Opposites: A list of ten fundamental pairs—such as Limit/Unlimited, Odd/Even, and Good/Bad—that defined the dualistic nature of the universe.

## Foundational Mathematical Contributions
Beyond the famous theorem, the Pythagorean school established several branches of study that became the basis for the classical Quadrivium (Arithmetic, Music, Geometry, and Astronomy).

* Geometry & The Theorem: While the Babylonians knew specific cases of $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$, the Pythagoreans are credited with transforming it into a universal formal proof.
* Number Theory: They classified numbers into categories such as odd and even, prime, perfect (equal to the sum of their divisors, like 6 or 28), and triangular.
* The Crisis of Irrationals: Their belief that all things could be expressed as ratios was shattered by their own discovery of irrational numbers (like $\sqrt{2}$, the diagonal of a unit square), which could not be written as a fraction.
* Platonic Solids: They discovered and studied the first three regular polyhedra (tetrahedron, cube, and dodecahedron), which they associated with the elements of the universe.

## Harmonics and the "Music of the Spheres"
The Pythagoreans were the first to apply experimental methods to music, discovering that harmonious intervals correspond to simple numerical ratios:

   1. Octave: 2:1 ratio.
   2. Perfect Fifth: 3:2 ratio.
   3. Perfect Fourth: 4:3 ratio.

This discovery led to the theory of Musica Universalis (Music of the Spheres), the belief that celestial bodies emit inaudible but harmonious sounds based on their speeds and distances from a "central fire".
## Cosmology and Ethics

* Non-Geocentric Model: The Pythagoreans were among the first to suggest the Earth was not the center of the universe, but instead orbited a Central Fire along with other planets and a hidden "Counter-Earth" (Antichthon).
* Metempsychosis: They believed the soul was immortal and underwent a cycle of reincarnation. Mathematical study was viewed as a form of "purification" to help the soul escape this cycle and achieve harmony with the divine.

## Branches & Disciplines (The Quadrivium)
The Pythagorean school formalized the four branches of study that would dominate Western education for 2,000 years. They viewed these not as separate subjects, but as four ways to view "Number."

* Arithmetic: Numbers at rest (Number Theory).
* Geometry: Numbers in space (Magnitude).
* Music (Harmonics): Numbers in time (Ratio & Proportion).
* Astronomy (Spherics): Numbers in space and time (Motion).

## Formulas & Theorems
Geometry & Trigonometry

* Pythagorean Theorem: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ (Relationship of sides in a right triangle).
* Reciprocal Pythagorean Theorem: $\frac{1}{a^2} + \frac{1}{b^2} = \frac{1}{h^2}$ (Where $h$ is the altitude to the hypotenuse).
* Euclidean Distance: $d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$ (Modern derivative).
* Pythagorean Identity: $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$.
* Internal Angles: First proof that the sum of angles in a triangle equals two right angles ($180^\circ$ or $\pi$ radians).

Number Theory Relations

* Triangular Numbers: $T_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$ (Sum of integers from 1 to $n$).
* Square Numbers: $S_n = n^2$ (Sum of first $n$ odd numbers).
* Perfect Numbers: $N = \sum (\text{proper divisors})$ (e.g., $6 = 1+2+3$).
* Amicable Numbers: Pairs where divisors of $A$ sum to $B$ and vice versa (e.g., 220 and 284).

## Algorithms & Methods
Generating Pythagorean Triples

* Odd Number Method (attributed to Pythagoras):
For any odd $m > 1$: $\left(m, \frac{m^2-1}{2}, \frac{m^2+1}{2}\right)$.
* Even Number Method (attributed to Plato/Pythagorean school):
For any even $m > 2$: $\left(m, (\frac{m}{2})^2 - 1, (\frac{m}{2})^2 + 1\right)$.

Approximation of Irrationals

* Side and Diagonal Numbers: An algorithm to approximate $\sqrt{2}$.
* Recursive definition: $S_{n+1} = S_n + D_n$ and $D_{n+1} = 2S_n + D_n$.
   * Ratio $\frac{D_n}{S_n}$ approaches $\sqrt{2}$ (e.g., 3/2, 7/5, 17/12, 41/29...).

Figurate Construction

* Gnomon Method: Generating larger squares by adding "L-shaped" gnomons (odd numbers) around a unit square.

## Ratios, Tuning & Standards
Musical Harmonics (The Diatonic Scale)
The Pythagoreans defined consonance through superparticular ratios ($n+1 : n$).

* Unison: 1:1
* Octave (Diapason): 2:1
* Perfect Fifth (Diapente): 3:2
* Perfect Fourth (Diatessaron): 4:3
* Whole Tone (Epogdoon): 9:8 (Difference between Fifth and Fourth).
* Pythagorean Comma: 531441:524288 (The minute discrepancy when stacking 12 perfect fifths vs. 7 octaves).

Geometric Constants

* $\sqrt{2}$: Discovered as the diagonal of a unit square. Its irrationality was a closely guarded secret that disrupted their doctrine.
* Golden Ratio ($\phi$): Found in the diagonals of the regular pentagon (the Pentagram).

## Logic & Classification Systems
The Table of Opposites (Systychia)
A binary logic system defining reality through ten fundamental dualities:

   1. Limit vs. Unlimited
   2. Odd vs. Even
   3. One vs. Many
   4. Right vs. Left
   5. Male vs. Female
   6. Rest vs. Motion
   7. Straight vs. Curved
   8. Light vs. Darkness
   9. Good vs. Evil
   10. Square vs. Oblong

Cosmological Logic

* Antichthon (Counter-Earth): A hypothetical body hypothesized to balance the universe to the "perfect" number 10.
* Central Fire: The gravitational center of the universe (not the Sun) around which Earth, Sun, and Counter-Earth orbited.

## Symbols & Associated Data
Sacred Symbols

* The Tetractys: A triangular figure of 10 points arranged in four rows ($1, 2, 3, 4$).
* Row 1: Unity (Monad).
   * Row 2: Duality (Dyad - Power/Limit).
   * Row 3: Plane (Triad - Harmony).
   * Row 4: Solid (Tetrad - Cosmos).
* The Pentagram (Pentalpha): Used as a secret recognition sign and symbol of health (Hygieia).
* Letter Y: Symbol of the "fork in the road" of life (Virtue vs. Vice).

Units of Reality (Metaphysical)

* Monad (1): The generator of all numbers; Reason; Stability.
* Dyad (2): Indefinite; Opinion; Matter.
* Triad (3): The first true number (beginning, middle, end).
* Tetrad (4): Justice; Squaring of accounts.
* Decad (10): Divine perfection; completion.

## Applications
Ancient

* Temple Architecture: Use of integer ratios to ensure structural harmony.

The Side and Diagonal numbers method, often called Theon's Ladder, is an ancient recursive algorithm used by the Pythagoreans to approximate $\sqrt{2}$. It relies on a sequence of integers where the ratio of a "Diagonal number" to a "Side number" gets closer to the actual value of $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.41421356...$ with every step.
## 1. The Recursive Formula
The method starts with an initial pair $s_0 = 1$ and $d_0 = 1$. To find the next pair in the sequence, use the following logic: 

* Next Side ($s_{n+1}$): $s_n + d_n$
* Next Diagonal ($d_{n+1}$): $2s_n + d_n$

## 2. Visualization of the Convergence
As the number of iterations increases, the ratio $\frac{d}{s}$ oscillates above and below the true value of $\sqrt{2}$, rapidly narrowing the gap.
## 3. Mathematical Logic
The logic behind this convergence is found in the identity $d_n^2 - 2s_n^2 = \pm 1$. Dividing by $s_n^2$ gives $(\frac{d_n}{s_n})^2 - 2 = \frac{\pm 1}{s_n^2}$. As $s_n$ grows larger, the term $\frac{\pm 1}{s_n^2}$ becomes effectively zero, forcing $(\frac{d_n}{s_n})^2 \approx 2$, which means $\frac{d_n}{s_n} \approx \sqrt{2}$. [1, 3] 
## Final Result
After 8 iterations, the Side and Diagonal method yields the ratio $577/408$, which results in the approximation $1.414216$. This is accurate to five decimal places compared to the true value of $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414214$. [5, 6] 

import pandas as pd
def generate_pythagorean_approximations(iterations):
    sides = [1]
    diagonals = [1]
    ratios = [1.0]
    
    for i in range(iterations - 1):
        s_next = sides[-1] + diagonals[-1]
        d_next = 2 * sides[-1] + diagonals[-1]
        sides.append(s_next)
        diagonals.append(d_next)
        ratios.append(d_next / s_next)
        
    df = pd.DataFrame({
        "Iteration": range(1, iterations + 1),
        "Side (s)": sides,
        "Diagonal (d)": diagonals,
        "Ratio (d/s)": ratios
    })
    return df

print(generate_pythagorean_approximations(8).to_string(index=False))
